The 4 Best Funiculars

1. Montmartre Funicular - Paris, France

Find it on Google Maps Here

Provides a nice pleasant stroll. The Montmartre funicular in Paris is an extremely popular funicular that offers amazing panoramic views of the beautiful city of Paris that no bus or car can ever hope to deliver. Using the funicular allows people to have almost a birds eye view of the wonderful city of Paris while also being close enough to the ground that you can still hear and feel the hustle of the city when compared to something like an aeroplane. Although the track first saw use in the year 1900, eventually the entire track was rebuilt in 1991 so it could utilize modern technology and allow itself to many more passengers on a daily basis. Currently the track boasts a full length of 108 meters as well as a vertical length of 36 meters.

2. Schwebebahn Dresden - Dresden, Germany

Find it on Google Maps Here

One of the oldest suspension railways in the world!!! Survived through WWII! The Schwebebahn Dresden funicular is Germany is a very modest funicular that often gets overshadowed by the nearby Schwebebahn Wuppertal, which is 13 kilometers long and is more stylish looking. However, the smaller Schwebebahn Dresden is a funicular that has a very unique and coveted history that speaks to the strength and longevity of the engineering behind it. The Schwebebahn Dresden is one of the oldest suspension railways in the world and has managed to remain in use and also survive throughout World War 2, a feat that very few funiculars around the world can boast of. Another unique aspect of the Schwebebahn Dresden can only be witnessed when paying close attention and notcing that instead of riding on tracks like most funiculars, the counterbalanced cars in this funicular are suspended from overhead girders or guide rails.

3. Gelmer Funicular - Switzerland

Find it on Google Maps Here

Steepest Funicular in Europe with an open top and a 106 degree incline! The Gelmer funicular in Switzerland is an equally beautiful and terrifying open top funicular that takes its passengers up to Lake Gelmer at a height of 448 meters. Just from looking at pictures of the funicular you can notice that it very much resembles rollercoasters that are commonly found in amusement parks however, this funicular has a much higher track rise than most rollercoasters, boasting a track rise of 448 meters as well as a length of 1028 meters long, with the steepest part of the rising incline being a 106 degree incline. Luckily for those with a fear of heights, the funicular does not travel as fast as a normal roller coaster, however this still does nothing to sooth the worries of passengers that have a fear of heights. Those who manage to control their fear of heights are rewarded with a beautiful birds eye view of the fresh and luscious vegetation of Switzerland.

4. Penang Hill Funicular Railway - Malaysia

Find it on Google Maps Here

Offers a breathtaking view aswell as a ride through a 260 meter tunnel. The Penang Hill Funicular Railway is a very popular tourist attraction in the beautiful country of Malaysia. This wonderful Funicular is located near the Kek Lok Si Temple which is another tourist attraction that is very spiritual and soothing. Originally it was created and opened as two-section railway in 1923 that saw extensive use until the entire design was overhauled and a more modern system and materials were used to help stabilize the the funicular and make it more safe. One very unique thing about this funicular is that it takes you through a massive 260 meter tunnel that will excite even the most inattentive and bored passengers.
